      ## Our vision
      <SubHeadline>
        Making complex genomic analysis accessible to researchers who study
        pathogens, their hosts, and vectors — regardless of computational
        background.
      </SubHeadline>
    </SectionHeadline>
    <SectionContent paired>
      <Grid gridSx={{ gap: 4 }}>
        <Accordion>
          <AccordionSummary>What we're building</AccordionSummary>
          <AccordionDetails>
            The biological questions that matter most — how a pathogen evolves
            under selection, which genes are differentially expressed during
            infection, how a new variant compares against reference strains —
            require integrating data, computation, and domain knowledge in ways
            that have historically demanded significant bioinformatics expertise.
            We believe that barrier should not exist.

            BRC Analytics is a platform that brings together:

            - **Curated genomic data** from NCBI, VEuPathDB, and other authoritative sources, organized by organism and assembly
            - **Ready-to-run workflows** built by the research community and distributed through the [Intergalactic Workflow Commission (IWC)](https://iwc.galaxyproject.org), pre-configured with the right reference files for each organism
            - **Free computational resources** via [UseGalaxy.org](https://usegalaxy.org), backed by TACC infrastructure, so researchers can run analyses at scale without institutional hardware
            - **Natural language tooling** that helps researchers translate a biological question into a runnable workflow
          </AccordionDetails>
        </Accordion>
        <Accordion>
          <AccordionSummary>Who we're building it for</AccordionSummary>
          <AccordionDetails>
            BRC Analytics supports researchers studying pathogens across the
            tree of life — viruses, bacteria, eukaryotic pathogens, helminths,
            vectors (including mosquitoes, ticks, and other arthropods), and
            their hosts. The platform is designed to be broadly useful across
            pathogen genomics, and we are actively expanding organism coverage.

            We prioritize researchers who:

            - Have domain expertise in their organism but limited bioinformatics infrastructure
            - Need reproducible, citable analyses they can share with collaborators or reference in publications
            - Want to move quickly from genomic data — whether from public databases or their own sequencing — to biological insight
          </AccordionDetails>
        </Accordion>
        <Accordion>
          <AccordionSummary>Our relationship with VEuPathDB</AccordionSummary>
          <AccordionDetails>
            BRC Analytics and [VEuPathDB](https://veupathdb.org) are
            complementary resources with a shared community. VEuPathDB offers
            genome browsing, gene pages, rich annotation, and a wealth of
            curated data for supported organisms. BRC Analytics focuses on the
            analysis side: a streamlined path from genomic data to running
            workflows and interpreting results.

            Our vision is that researchers move fluidly between the two. We
            import foundational data and gene models from VEuPathDB so that BRC
            Analytics users are working with the same annotations their
            community relies on. And we are building a route for researchers to
            take their analysis results — differential expression outputs,
            variant calls, custom gene lists — back into VEuPathDB as user
            datasets, completing the circle. We see the two resources as entry
            points into the same ecosystem, not alternatives to each other.
          </AccordionDetails>
        </Accordion>
        <Accordion>
          <AccordionSummary>Our approach</AccordionSummary>
          <AccordionDetails>
            We are not building a closed system. Every curated workflow we
            surface comes from the open IWC catalog, and researchers can also
            bring their own data directly into Galaxy and run any tool or
            workflow available there. Our data is drawn from public NCBI
            releases and VEuPathDB. Our compute runs on UseGalaxy.org, which is
            itself open-source and publicly accessible.

            We believe the future of pathogen genomics infrastructure is
            community-driven: researchers contribute workflows, curate reference
            data, and share reproducible analyses. BRC Analytics is designed to
            be a platform where that community can thrive.
          </AccordionDetails>
        </Accordion>
        <Accordion>
          <AccordionSummary>Our commitments</AccordionSummary>
          <AccordionDetails>
            - **Free to use**: Funded by NIAID, no fees for access, analysis, or storage within standard allocations
            - **Open source**: Built on open platforms; our code is [publicly available on GitHub](https://github.com/galaxyproject/brc-analytics)
            - **Organism-aware**: Data, workflows, and reference assets are curated at the species level, not one-size-fits-all
            - **Reproducible**: Every analysis preserves full provenance in Galaxy so results can be reproduced and shared

            See the [Roadmap](/about/roadmap) for what we are actively building
            next, or reach out at [help@brc-analytics.org](mailto:help@brc-analytics.org)
            with questions or feature requests.
          </AccordionDetails>
        </Accordion>
